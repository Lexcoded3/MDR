📘 CHAPTER ONE: INTRODUCTION
1.1 Background of the Study

Tuberculosis (TB) remains one of the leading infectious diseases worldwide, with increasing cases of drug-resistant strains such as Multidrug-resistant tuberculosis. These cases are particularly challenging to treat due to resistance to key medications like Rifampicin.

Effective treatment of GxAlert requires strict adherence to medication schedules over a prolonged period. However, many patients fail to consistently follow prescribed regimens due to forgetfulness, lack of monitoring, and limited follow-up from healthcare providers.

With advancements in Information Technology, digital health systems can be utilized to improve treatment adherence through automated reminders, monitoring, and reporting. This study proposes the development of an GxAlert Patient Adherence and Reminder System to support healthcare providers in managing patient treatment and improving outcomes.

1.2 Problem Statement

Despite the availability of treatment protocols for GxAlert, patient non-adherence to medication schedules remains a major challenge. Missed doses can lead to prolonged illness, increased transmission, and further drug resistance.

Currently, many healthcare facilities rely on manual tracking methods, which are inefficient and prone to errors. There is a need for an automated system that can:

Remind patients to take medication
Track adherence levels
Support healthcare workers with timely data
1.3 Objectives
1.3.1 General Objective

To develop a system that improves medication adherence among GxAlert patients through automated reminders and monitoring.

1.3.2 Specific Objectives
To design a database for managing patient and treatment data
To develop a system for sending automated reminders
To track and record patient adherence
To generate reports for healthcare providers
To support multiple users with different roles
1.4 Scope of the Study

The system will focus on:

GxAlert patient management
Medication reminder scheduling
Adherence tracking
Role-based access (clinicians, nurses, lab personnel, data officers)

The system will not cover:

Full hospital management
Advanced diagnostics
1.5 Significance of the Study

This system will:

Improve patient adherence
Assist healthcare workers in monitoring patients
Reduce missed doses
Provide reliable data for decision-making
📘 CHAPTER THREE: SYSTEM DESIGN
3.1 System Overview

The proposed system is a web-based application designed to manage GxAlert patients, schedule medication reminders, and monitor adherence. It supports multiple users including clinicians, nurses, lab personnel, and data officers.
